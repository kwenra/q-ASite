College of Arts and Sciences

Course Name Instructor Summary Other Information Reviews
Introduction to Dance as a Contemporary Art Form David Marchant Introduction to dance as a creative art form. Students gain an understanding of the body through movement and expression. Open to all students
  • "It's a super chill class! You get to roll around on the ground and I don't know what could be better than that." - Eve
  • "A fantastic dancer and a helpful, wonderful teacher. I never once felt self-conscious - he sees beauty and potential in every dancer." - Jay
Principles of Human Anatomy and Development David Strait An anatomy class that covers most of the functional systems of the human body. Offers valuable preparation to any student interested in human biology, health sciences, medicine, and anthropology.
  • "Makes the worst (read: best) jokes ever. Also his wife calls him during class a lot and we get to say hi." - Yuu
  • "Very interesting lectures. Easy to pay attention to and easy to take notes from what he says. Teaches with great clarity on the subject." - Juanita

School of Engineering and Applied Sciences

Course Name Instructor Summary Other Information Reviews
Web Development Simon Yost A course that explores the process of designing, creating, and publishing a website. This course integrates basic design principles that graphic designers use alongside the HTML and CSS languages. No experience necessary! "Really knows his stuff! Tries to cover a lot of material which may seem like a lot but it's very helpful material!" - Kevin
Computer Science I Cytron A course that teaches the basics of computer programming. Uses the Java language. No experience necessary! Online course with lab and studio sections that one must be present at.
  • "Professor Cytron made computer science really fun, and found a relatively easy way to teach a pretty hard topic." - Lay
  • "Cognizant of student's needs regarding scheduling and approaches to learning. Highly recommend taking his class!!" - Eugene
